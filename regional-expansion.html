Regional League Expansion

Infrastructure-first development of professional esports beyond traditional coastal hubs.

Current Landscape

Professional esports leagues in North America are heavily concentrated in coastal regions, while competitive participation and viewership continue to grow rapidly in non-coastal markets.

The Midwest has demonstrated sustained grassroots and collegiate growth, yet remains underrepresented at the professional league level.

Midwest as a Proof Market

  • • Established collegiate and high school esports programs
  • • Strong participation without proportional pro representation
  • • Lower operational costs compared to coastal markets
  • • Existing broadcast and competition infrastructure

The Midwest serves as a viable proving ground for scalable professional league systems outside legacy esports hubs.

Extension Into Central America

As esports participation continues to expand globally, similar growth patterns are emerging in Central America. These regions show competitive interest and player development potential, but limited access to professional league pathways.

  • • Growing competitive player base
  • • Digital-first access to competition and broadcast
  • • Opportunity for remote league models
  • • Natural extension of non-coastal expansion strategies

Infrastructure-First Approach

  • • Remote-capable broadcast and competition systems
  • • Standardized league operations and scheduling
  • • Production pipelines designed for low-overhead scaling
  • • Talent visibility through consistent competitive presentation

This approach prioritizes sustainable systems over location-dependent investment, allowing competitive ecosystems to grow before physical expansion.

Current Status

This initiative is currently in the research and planning phase, informed by ongoing collegiate esports operations, broadcast production leadership, and technical infrastructure work.

No formal league deployment is underway at this time.
